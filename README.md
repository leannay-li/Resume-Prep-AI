# Build Your Resume with AI: Complete Job Search Preparation System

**What this is:** A modular skill system for honest, systematic job search preparation. Learn about your projects, identify gaps vs target roles, deepen your understanding, and test yourself under pressure.

**What this is NOT:** Resume beautification, misleading claims, or optimization tricks. This system is about self-discovery and deep learning.

**How to use:** Pick a workflow below. Each invokes specific skills in order. Mix and match based on your timeline and goals.

---

## Quick Tour: What Each Skill Does

### Foundation Skills (Know Yourself)

**[`/new-grad-resume-guide`](skills/new-grad-resume-guide/README.md)** — Resume strategy for zero professional experience
- How to structure a resume when you don't have jobs
- What counts as "experience" (projects, school work, open source)
- How to avoid common new grad mistakes

**[`/experienced-resume-guide`](skills/experienced-resume-guide/README.md)** — Resume strategy for 1–5 years experience
- How to shift from listing duties to showing impact
- When to quantify (always) vs estimate vs describe
- How to position your background for your target role

### Expression Skills (Build Your Materials)

**[`/summary-writer`](skills/summary-writer/README.md)** — Write your positioning summary
- 2-3 lines that say who you are and why hire you
- Different summaries for different roles
- Interactive brainstorming to find the right angle

**[`/summary-reviewer`](skills/summary-reviewer/README.md)** — Get hiring manager feedback on your summary
- Would a hiring manager take a meeting based on this?
- Tests: Clarity, Positioning, Strength, Tone
- Before/After suggestions

**[`/bullet-writer`](skills/bullet-writer/README.md)** — Transform projects into 3–5 resume bullets
- Action + Technical Detail + Measurable Impact formula
- Helps you quantify impact honestly
- Bullets work across multiple resumes

**[`/bullet-reviewer`](skills/bullet-reviewer/README.md)** — Optimize bullets for specific job descriptions
- Strategic keyword alignment and emphasis
- Shows how to transfer skills (AWS→GCP, React→Vue)
- Surgical tweaks, not rewrites

### Meta Skills (Organize Your System)

**[`/resume-system-orchestrator`](skills/resume-system-orchestrator/README.md)** — Build a modular resume system
- How to organize summaries, bullets, education, skills
- Create multiple tailored resumes without duplicating work
- File organization and maintenance approach

### Deep Work Skills (Master Your Story)

**[`/case-study-generator`](skills/case-study-generator/README.md)** — Expand ONE bullet into a complete case study
- Full STAR (Situation, Task, Action, Result) narrative
- Honesty audit: Solid ✅ / Inflated 🟡 / Unclear ❓
- Foundation for deep interview prep

**[`/gap-analyzer`](skills/gap-analyzer/README.md)** — Compare your project to a target job description
- 6-part diagnostic: Relevance, Gaps, Enhanced Design, Learning Checklist, Timeline, Before/After
- Shows realistic way to deepen your project
- Generates your learning roadmap

**[`/concept-mastery-guide`](skills/concept-mastery-guide/README.md)** — Learn concepts deeply through thought experiments
- Plain-language explanation + project connection + edge cases
- Study notes for every concept
- 🟢 Already know / 🟡 Know basics, need practice / 🔴 Completely new

**[`/mock-interview`](skills/mock-interview/README.md)** — Realistic mock interview under pressure
- No teaching, no hints. Real technical questions.
- Assessment report: ✅ Ready / ⏳ Borderline / ❌ Needs work
- Learn-test loop: Study → Test → Identify gaps → Study again

## 3 Workflows: Pick Your Path

### Workflow A: Quick Resume (2–3 hours)

**For:** Someone who needs a polished resume quickly for upcoming applications

**Timeline:** Can do in one session

**Skills used:**
1. `/new-grad-resume-guide` or `/experienced-resume-guide` (15 min) — Pick your structure
2. `/summary-writer` (15 min) — Write 2-3 positioning summaries
3. `/summary-reviewer` (10 min) — Get feedback, refine once
4. `/bullet-writer` (30 min per project, 2-3 projects) — Write bullets for main projects
5. `/resume-system-orchestrator` (15 min) — Assemble final resume

**Output:** Complete resume ready to apply with

**When to use:** 
- You have interviews coming up and need a polished resume
- You know your stories and just need to write them clearly
- You're not doing deep interview prep yet

**Realistic expectations:**
- Resume will be honest and well-organized
- Not optimized for every single job description
- Good foundation, not a finalized masterpiece

---

### Workflow B: Deep Mastery (4–6 hours, spread over 1–2 weeks)

**For:** Someone serious about interview prep. Wants to understand what they actually know vs what needs learning.

**Timeline:** Spread over 1–2 weeks (not one sitting)

**Skills used:**
1. `/case-study-generator` (45 min) — Pick one strong project, expand it fully
2. `/gap-analyzer` (60 min) — Compare to target JD, get learning checklist
3. `/concept-mastery-guide` (1–2 hours per concept, 3–5 concepts) — Study key concepts deeply
4. `/mock-interview` (30–45 min) — Test your understanding under pressure
5. `/bullet-writer` or `/bullet-reviewer` (optional) — Refine bullets based on what you learned

**Output:** 
- Deep understanding of your projects
- Identified learning gaps
- Study notes for interview prep
- Real sense of what you know vs don't

**When to use:**
- You have 1–2 weeks before interviews
- You want to know you're truly prepared
- You want honest feedback on gaps before the real interview

**Realistic expectations:**
- You'll find things you don't understand (that's the point)
- The learning roadmap may take more than 4–6 hours if gaps are large
- You'll feel more confident because you've tested yourself

---

### Workflow C: Complete System (Full Course, 2–4 weeks)

**For:** Someone doing serious job search prep. Want the complete system: honest self-knowledge, tailored materials, deep understanding, real interview readiness.

**Timeline:** 2–4 weeks

**Skills used (all 11):**
1. `/new-grad-resume-guide` or `/experienced-resume-guide` — Understand resume strategy
2. `/summary-writer` → `/summary-reviewer` — Create multiple positioning summaries
3. `/bullet-writer` → `/bullet-reviewer` — Write and optimize bullets
4. `/resume-system-orchestrator` — Organize everything into modular system
5. `/case-study-generator` (2–3 projects) — Deep dive on your strong projects
6. `/gap-analyzer` (per target JD) — Identify learning gaps per role
7. `/concept-mastery-guide` (multiple concepts) — Master the gaps
8. `/mock-interview` (multiple rounds) — Test, identify, test again
9. Refine based on feedback (may loop back)

**Output:**
- Complete, well-organized resume system
- Multiple case studies you can speak to confidently
- Learning roadmaps for each target role
- Study notes on all key concepts
- Real interview readiness (not just hoping)

**When to use:**
- You're doing a serious job search
- You're targeting specific companies/roles
- You want systematic rather than ad-hoc prep
- You want to feel genuinely ready, not just hopeful

**Realistic expectations:**
- This is more work (but worth it)
- You'll discover gaps that require study
- By end, you'll have deep understanding + proven materials
- The system stays with you for future jobs

## Principles Across All Skills

### 1. Honesty Over Optimization

Every skill emphasizes honesty:
- `/case-study-generator` uses honesty audit (Solid/Inflated/Unclear)
- `/bullet-writer` helps you quantify or acknowledge estimates
- `/gap-analyzer` tells you if your project is relevant or if you should pick a different one
- `/mock-interview` gives you unfiltered feedback

This isn't about making claims look better. It's about knowing what you truly understand.

### 2. Project-Specific Always

Nothing happens in a vacuum:
- Bullets come from specific projects
- Case studies go deep on specific work
- Gap analysis compares your project to specific JDs
- Concept learning connects to your specific system

Abstract without concrete context doesn't teach; project context unlocks everything.

### 3. Measurable > Estimated > Qualitative

Impact hierarchy (honest version):
- 🔴 Red = Measured (you have data: metrics, deployed, users, revenue)
- 🟡 Orange = Estimated (reasonable guess: based on effort or scope)
- 🟢 Green = Qualitative (description: "built," "improved")

You can claim all three, but label which is which. Interviewers respect honesty.

### 4. Skill Pairing Works

Each skill stands alone OR pairs with others:
- Use `/summary-writer` by itself for quick help
- OR use `/summary-writer` → `/summary-reviewer` for full loop
- `/case-study-generator` alone = understand one project
- `/case-study-generator` → `/gap-analyzer` → `/concept-mastery-guide` → `/mock-interview` = deep prep

No skill is orphaned. They fit together.

### 5. Learning Over Luck

Interview readiness comes from:
- Understanding what you know (case study generator)
- Knowing what you don't know (gap analyzer)
- Fixing the gaps (concept mastery guide)
- Testing yourself (mock interview)

Not hope. Not luck. Knowledge.

## Tips for Success

### General Tips

- **Do one skill at a time.** Don't try all 11 at once.
- **One concept, one bullet, one interview at a time.** Depth over breadth.
- **Save your work.** Each skill generates something useful (notes, bullets, study materials).
- **The system is modular.** Use it flexibly. You don't have to do everything.
- **Pause between intensive skills.** `/concept-mastery-guide` is deep; take a break before `/mock-interview`.

### For Interview Prep (Workflows B & C)

- **Start with your strongest project.** Use `/case-study-generator` on work you're most confident about.
- **Be honest in gap analysis.** If the gaps are huge, maybe pick a different project or pick a different JD.
- **Study 3–5 concepts before mocking.** Don't go straight to `/mock-interview` without studying.
- **Mock interview is realistic.** It will feel hard. That's intentional.
- **Fail safely here.** Mock failures are gifts. You're learning what you don't know before the real interview.
- **Loop: Study → Mock → Identify gaps → Study → Mock again.** Repeat until confident.

### For Resume Building (Workflow A)

- **Start with `/summary-writer`.** Your summary is your positioning hook.
- **Write bullets for 2–3 strong projects.** Not everything, just your best work.
- **Use `/bullet-reviewer` if targeting a specific JD.** Otherwise generic bullets work fine.
- **Test your resume with real applications.** Does it get interviews? If not, refine.

### For System Building (Workflow C)

- **Organize as you go.** Use `/resume-system-orchestrator` early, not at the end.
- **Create multiple case studies** (2–3 projects). Not everything, just depth on strong ones.
- **Per target role:** Run `/gap-analyzer` on your case study vs their JD. Different roles = different gaps.
- **Batch learning:** Study all concepts for one role, mock that role, then move to next role.

## FAQ

**Q: Do I have to use all 11 skills?**
A: No. Use what fits your needs. Workflow A uses 5 skills. Workflow B uses 8 skills. Workflow C uses all 11.

**Q: Can I use these skills for other job searches?**
A: Absolutely. The system is modular and reusable. Save your materials. Next job search, update and reuse.

**Q: What if I discover I don't understand something in mock-interview?**
A: Perfect. That's the point. Go back to `/concept-mastery-guide` for that gap. Study it. Mock again.

**Q: How honest should I be about what I don't know?**
A: Completely honest. That's the whole system. "I'm not sure" → Learn → Be sure.

**Q: Should I do multiple mock interviews?**
A: Yes, if you want deep practice. Do one, review feedback, study gaps, do another. Not all in one day. Spread them out.

**Q: This seems like a lot of work.**
A: It is. And it's worth it. Compare: Spending 20 hours on interview prep vs getting a job that pays 6 figures. The ROI is insane.

**Q: Can I skip `/case-study-generator` and go straight to `/gap-analyzer`?**
A: Not really. Case study is the input to gap analyzer. They're a pair.

**Q: What if `/gap-analyzer` says my project is "weakly relevant"?**
A: Pick a different project or different target role. The system is honest—if it's a bad fit, better to know now.

## Building This System (Meta Context)

This skill system was built from a "Build Your Resume with AI" course. The course taught 8 lessons:

1. Resume strategy (new grad vs experienced)
2. Positioning (summary writing)
3. Impact extraction (bullet writing)
4. Organization (modular resume system)
5. Project understanding (case studies)
6. Gap analysis (interview prep)
7. Concept mastery (deep learning)
8. Interview simulation (mock interviews)

These 8 lessons were expanded into 11 reusable Claude Code skills, organized into 4 tiers:
- **Foundation (2 skills):** Resume strategy guides
- **Expression (4 skills):** Summary + bullet writing
- **Meta (1 skill):** System organization
- **Deep Work (4 skills):** Case studies, gaps, learning, testing

The system emphasizes:
- **Honesty** — Audit your claims, don't inflate
- **Depth** — Understand your work deeply, not surface-level
- **Modularity** — Reuse materials across jobs and roles
- **Learning** — Know what you don't know, then learn it
- **Testing** — Test yourself before the real interview

This isn't resume beautification. It's systematic self-discovery for job search success.
