# Summary Writer

**For:** Anyone building a resume summary  
**Use when:** You need a compelling 2-3 line positioning statement for a specific role direction  
**Invocation:** `/summary-writer`

## What It Does

Helps you write a direction-specific resume summary through interactive brainstorming. Not a generic introduction—a POSITIONING STATEMENT.

Key insight: Same person can have different summaries for different role directions (Startup SDE vs Enterprise SDE vs ML Engineer all positioned differently).

## When to Use It

✅ **Use this if:**
- You're targeting a specific role direction
- You want a summary that stands out (not generic)
- You want to clarify your positioning before writing bullets
- You want something 2-3 lines that makes someone want to read your bullets

❌ **Don't use if:**
- You're not sure what role direction you want (explore first, then come back)
- You don't want a summary (some resumes are better without one)
- You want a generic summary that "works for everything" (won't work—be specific)

## How to Use It

1. Run `/summary-writer`
2. Have ready:
   - Your background (years of experience, roles, key wins)
   - Target role direction (startup SDE? data engineer? ML? product?)
   - Your core strength (what makes you different?)
   - Career aspirations (where do you want to go?)
3. Engage with the interactive brainstorming
4. Get a polished, direction-specific summary
5. Next: `/summary-reviewer` to get feedback

## What You Get

A professional 2-3 line summary like:

> "Backend engineer with 3 years building API-driven systems at startups. Strong in microservices architecture and database optimization. Looking to grow toward technical leadership."

Key qualities:
- ✅ Direction-specific (signals fit for this role type)
- ✅ Shows your differentiator (not generic)
- ✅ Establishes positioning (what you're known for)
- ✅ Professional but natural
- ✅ Makes someone want to read your bullets

## The Formula

**[Who you are] [What you do best] [Why hire you]**

Examples:
- "Backend engineer focused on X. Y years at Z. Strong in A and B."
- "Data engineer building analytical platforms. Expert at X. Passionate about Y."
- "Full-stack developer shipping fast. Experienced in X and Y. Track record at startups."

## Key Principle

**Same person ≠ same summary for all roles**

Your summary changes based on direction:
- **For Startup:** Emphasize "ships fast," "wears many hats," "moves quickly"
- **For Enterprise:** Emphasize "scalable systems," "documentation," "collaboration"
- **For Data:** Emphasize "insights," "pipelines," "analytics"

## Tips

1. **Be specific** — Not "experienced engineer" but "3-year backend engineer focused on microservices"
2. **Show your differentiator** — What makes you different? Own it
3. **Direction matters** — Different summaries for different role types
4. **Read it aloud** — Does it sound like you? Adjust
5. **Short and punchy** — 2-3 lines max. If it's longer, cut

## Next Step

Once you have a summary → `/summary-reviewer` (get feedback from a hiring manager's perspective)
