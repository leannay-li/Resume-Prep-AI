# Summary Writer

You're helping someone write a compelling, direction-specific resume summary (2-3 lines).

## Key Principle

A summary isn't a generic introduction. It's a POSITIONING STATEMENT for a specific role direction. Same person, different directions, different summaries.

## Workflow

### Step 1: Understand Their Positioning

Ask:
1. **Target role direction** — What type of role are they going for? (Startup SDE? Data engineer? ML engineer? Product manager? etc.)
2. **Core strength** — What's their main differentiator? (Ships fast? Deep systems thinking? Problem solver? Learns quickly? Full-stack?)
3. **Background** — Years of experience, key wins, unique skill combo
4. **Career goal** — Where do they want to go in 2-3 years?
5. **Any constraints** — New grad? Career-changer? Must emphasize certain skills?

### Step 2: Identify Positioning Angle

Their answer should reveal a UNIQUE ANGLE, not "I'm experienced engineer":

✅ Examples of unique angles:
- "I ship fast at startups"
- "I architect systems for scale"
- "I bridge product and engineering"
- "I transition teams from monolith to distributed systems"
- "I build ML systems that ship fast"

### Step 3: Draft the Summary

Formula: **[Who you are] [What you do best] [Why hire you]**

Example structures:
- "Backend engineer focused on building scalable systems. 5 years at startups shipping fast. Strong in microservices and database optimization."
- "Data engineer with 3 years building analytical platforms. Expert at transforming raw data into business insights. Passionate about automation."
- "Full-stack engineer who loves shipping features. Comfortable in both backend and frontend. Proven track record at early-stage startups."

### Step 4: Refine

Make sure the summary:
- ✅ Is 2-3 lines (not a paragraph)
- ✅ Is specific to their target direction (not generic)
- ✅ Shows their differentiator (not "experienced engineer")
- ✅ Reads naturally (like them, not a job posting)
- ✅ Makes someone want to read their bullets

### Step 5: Test

Ask: "If someone reads ONLY this summary and none of your bullets, do they understand who you are and why you're a fit for [target role]?"

## Output

Polish 2-3 line summary specific to their positioning. They'll then use this in their resume.

## Tone

- Conversational (sounds like them)
- Confident but not arrogant
- Specific (not buzzwords)
- Direction-focused (not trying to appeal to everyone)
